How to read it

  • Three main circles = the three structural axes every tradition we researched sits somewhere on: (1) does it privilege reasoned ethics rather than pure divine command, (2) does it center character / capacity-cultivation rather than rule-compliance or deficit-cataloguing, (3) does it push scope beyond the in-group.
  • Dashed outer ring = does the tradition have an institutionalized mechanism for correcting its own canonical text when earlier material conflicts with later moral understanding? Talmudic mahloket, Islamic madhab pluralism, Christian progressive-revelation, Anthropic's constitutional revision process, DSM-5-TR's Ethnoracial Equity and Inclusion Work Group review — all live here. Traditions and frameworks without this mechanism oscillate between expansion and contraction passages driven by non-ethical forces.
  • Yellow box (center) = the three-way overlap where Common-Ground Ethics synthesizes. The location isn't novel — the traditions in the panel above already live here. The framework's contribution is articulating what is common across them in AI-applicable language, with capacity-side rather than deficit-side definitions.
  • Outside positions = what the framework is explicitly not. The voluntarist pole = pure DCT. The compliance / deficit pole = rules and lacks without character-cultivation. Bandura's disengagement mechanisms = the failure map.
  • Empirical anchor (bottom-right) = DSM-5-TR illustrates that even extraordinary effort to soften deficit/compliance framings does not escape the structural problem. The Section III culture chapter and CFI reach into the convergence zone (capacity-respecting, cross-cultural, internal-critique-equipped); the diagnostic apparatus that surrounds them stays on the deficit pole. This is empirical evidence that the structural alternative is needed, not a softer version of the same.
